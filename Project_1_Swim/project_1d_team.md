## Purpose of the ML Component (Team)
The goal of the ML Component is for each student to first write an individual paper summary. The main text, written collaboratively as a team, should then clearly present the problem formulation, the learning-based approach, and the expected findings, and explain how the proposed method relates to classical algorithms. There is no code deliverable for this component; students are expected to submit a literature review and a research proposal.

---


### Introduction (10 pts)

The introduction should begin with a clear and concise statement of the problem, explaining why the task is important and framing it as a research question with measurable success criteria (e.g., specific evaluation metric or dataset split). It should then include a one-sentence summary of the core approach, highlighting how the proposed method differs from classical algorithms. Finally, the paragraph should discuss the anticipated impact of the work, identifying the target beneficiaries, the research gap being addressed, and expected implications such as improved generalization or deployment safety.

### Related Work (10 pts)

This section must cite 5 or 6 (depends on group size) papers in total (with BibTeX entries), corresponding to the individual summaries in the Appendix. The paragraph briefly describing the methodologies used and their limitations in a very high level.

### Method (10 pts)

This section should begin with a decomposition of the problem into components, along with clear hypotheses that describe the expected outcomes and are tied to specific evaluation metrics. It should then describe the core model or algorithm, including relevant architectural details, loss functions, regularization techniques, augmentations, optimization method, and batch size. Finally, the paragraph should detail the evaluation protocol, including the datasets and their splits, data source, size, modalities, and any known biases or limitations. It should also define the primary metrics used to assess performance.

### Potential Experiments (10 pts)

This section should identify one strong and justified baseline, which may be classical or a prior state-of-the-art method, and explain why it is a fair comparison. The paragraph should also outline a set of experiments and ablation studies designed to test the hypotheses stated earlier. These should include variations of model components or inputs to confirm or refute the claimed contributions.

### Appendix (10 pts)

The appendix should contain the individual summaries for each student in the team. As we mentioned, each student will read one paper and write about a quarter to half a page summarizing the paper's contribution, the problem it is addressing, the approach it takes, and something about the results.
